Butter Chicken Kofta Masala

Rich and creamy butter chicken kofta masala with minced chicken balls in a lusci...

4-5 servings
30 min + 35 min

Ingredients

  • 1/2 kg chicken keema (mince)
  • Whole garam masala - shahjeera, cloves, cardamom, black pepper, 2 large cardamom, bay leaf, cinnamon
  • Ginger garlic paste - 1 tablespoon (for kofta)
  • Oil for frying koftas
  • For gravy paste: 2 medium onions, roughly chopped
  • 2 green chillies
  • Ginger garlic paste - 1 tablespoon (for gravy)
  • 2 tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 10-12 cashews, boiled
  • Coriander powder - 1/2 tablespoon
  • Turmeric - 1/2 tablespoon
  • Salt - 1/2 tablespoon
  • Red chilli powder - 1/2 tablespoon
  • Cumin - 1/2 tablespoon
  • Oil - 2 tablespoons (for gravy)
  • Butter
  • 1 large onion, chopped (for final gravy)
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • 1 glass warm water

Directions

  1. 1.Wash the chicken mince, add whole garam masala (shahjeera, cloves, cardamom, black pepper, large cardamom, bay leaf, cinnamon) and grind everything together into a smooth paste.
  2. 2.Add 1 tablespoon ginger garlic paste to the ground mince and mix well.
  3. 3.Grease hands with oil and shape the mince into round kofta balls.
  4. 4.In a pan with a little oil, add the koftas and cover. Steam-cook for 5 minutes, then add a little more oil and fry until golden.
  5. 5.For the gravy paste: cook 2 onions, 2 green chillies, 1 tablespoon ginger garlic paste, 2 tomatoes, and boiled cashews together until soft.
  6. 6.Add coriander powder, turmeric, salt, red chilli powder, and cumin. Cook until done, then cool and blend into a smooth paste.
  7. 7.In a pan, heat oil and butter. Fry 1 chopped onion until soft. Add the blended gravy paste and cook for 5 minutes until oil separates.
  8. 8.Add the fried koftas to the gravy. Mix gently, cover and cook for 5 minutes.
  9. 9.Add 1 glass warm water, chopped coriander leaves, mix well. Adjust salt and spices.
  10. 10.Cover and cook for another 5 minutes. Add melted butter on top, mix gently, and let it rest covered for 5 minutes before serving.

Tips

  • *Grease hands with oil to easily shape koftas
  • *Steam the koftas first before frying for better texture
  • *Use warm water when adding to gravy for better consistency
  • *Keep the gravy slightly thick for best results
