Restaurant Style Kofta Masala

Perfect soft and juicy kofta masala in restaurant style, made with beef koftas c...

4-6 servings
20 min + 35 min

Ingredients

  • For kofta: 1/2 kg boneless beef
  • Salt, cumin powder, red chilli powder, coriander powder, turmeric, garam masala - 1/2 tablespoon each
  • Ginger garlic paste - 1/2 tablespoon
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • Oil, mint, and fresh coriander
  • For gravy: 2 medium onions, fried (birista)
  • Dried coconut, sliced and fried
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • Yogurt
  • Ginger garlic paste - 1 tablespoon
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • Fresh coriander
  • Red chilli powder, salt, cumin powder, turmeric, coriander powder, garam masala
  • Oil - 1 tablespoon
  • Hot water (for gravy)

Directions

  1. 1.Grind beef with dry masalas, ginger garlic paste, onion, oil, mint, and coriander in a chopper.
  2. 2.Shape into kofta balls. Place in a dry pan without oil, add 1 teaspoon oil on top, cook on low for 10 minutes (they will cook in their own juices).
  3. 3.Once water dries, add a little oil and mix for 1-2 minutes.
  4. 4.For gravy, grind fried onions (birista) and fried coconut into a paste.
  5. 5.Heat oil, fry chopped onion and ginger garlic paste, add dry masalas.
  6. 6.Add tomato puree and yogurt, cook well. Add hot water as needed.
  7. 7.Add the koftas and the birista-coconut paste. Add hot water for gravy.
  8. 8.Cook on low flame for 5 minutes, add coriander, cover and rest for 5 minutes.

Tips

  • *Koftas cook in their own juices without deep frying
  • *Fried onion (birista) and coconut paste gives the gravy its richness
  • *Keep flame low to prevent the gravy from sticking

Assalamu Alaikum. No need to go to a restaurant. Today I am going to teach you perfect, soft and juicy kofta masala that will be completely hotel style. So let's start making it.
