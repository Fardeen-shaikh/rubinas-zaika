Cheesy Chicken Pocket

Crispy chicken cheese pockets made with marinated chicken keema, a creamy cheese...

18-20 servings
40 min + 25 min

Ingredients

  • 1/2 kg chicken keema (mince), washed
  • 1 tablespoon ginger garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon cumin powder
  • 1 tablespoon red chilli powder
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper powder
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons dark soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons oil (for cooking keema)
  • Butter (for sauce)
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida, for sauce)
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper powder (for sauce)
  • 1 tablespoon salt (for sauce)
  • 1 tablespoon oregano (optional)
  • 1/2 cup cream (malai)
  • Mozzarella cheese (to taste)
  • Samosa patti (strips)
  • Maida and water slurry (for sealing)
  • Oil for deep frying

Directions

  1. 1.Wash 1/2 kg chicken keema. Add 1 tablespoon ginger garlic paste, cumin powder, red chilli powder, salt, black pepper powder, 2 tablespoons vinegar, and 2 tablespoons dark soy sauce. Mix well and marinate for 30 minutes.
  2. 2.Heat 2 teaspoons oil in a pan. Add the marinated keema and cook until fully done, mixing well. Set aside.
  3. 3.For the sauce: melt butter in a pan on low flame. Add 4 tablespoons maida and stir until slightly browned with no lumps.
  4. 4.Gradually add 2 cups milk, stirring continuously to avoid lumps. Add 1 tablespoon black pepper, 1 tablespoon salt, and 1 tablespoon oregano. Mix well and cook on low flame.
  5. 5.Add 1/2 cup cream (malai) and mix. Then add mozzarella cheese and stir until melted and the sauce becomes thick and creamy.
  6. 6.Add the cooked chicken keema to the cheese sauce and mix well. Turn off the flame and let the filling cool completely.
  7. 7.Arrange samosa patti strips in a plus/cross shape. Place filling in the center. Apply maida-water slurry on the edges. Fold all four sides to form a box/pocket shape.
  8. 8.Heat oil for deep frying. Once hot, reduce flame to low. Fry 2-3 pockets at a time until golden and crispy on both sides.
  9. 9.Serve hot with sauce or mayonnaise.

Tips

  • *Marinate the keema for at least 30 minutes for best flavour
  • *Keep the flame low while making the white sauce to avoid lumps
  • *Let the filling cool completely before making pockets — warm filling will tear the patti
  • *500g chicken keema makes about 20 pockets
  • *Fry on low flame after initial heating for even, crispy results
