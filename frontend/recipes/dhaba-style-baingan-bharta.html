Dhaba Style Baingan Bharta

Quick and delicious dhaba-style baingan bharta (smoky roasted eggplant mash). Ev...

3-4 servings
10 min + 20 min

Ingredients

  • 500g brinjal/eggplant (2 large)
  • Oil (for rubbing on brinjal)
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1/2 tablespoon mustard seeds (rai)
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 10-12 garlic cloves
  • 1 small piece of ginger
  • 3 green chillies
  • 1/2 tablespoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon red chilli powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • A little water
  • Fresh coriander, chopped
  • 1/2 tablespoon garam masala powder
  • 2 teaspoons oil (for cooking)

Directions

  1. 1.Rub oil all over the brinjals and spread evenly (this helps skin peel off easily). Make a slit in the centre to check the brinjal is not spoiled inside.
  2. 2.Roast the brinjals directly on a flame (use a wire rack or place directly on gas). Turn occasionally until fully charred and soft on all sides.
  3. 3.Meanwhile, prepare the masala: heat 2 teaspoons oil in a pan. Add 1 tablespoon cumin and 1/2 tablespoon mustard seeds. Fry briefly.
  4. 4.Add 2 finely chopped onions and fry until golden.
  5. 5.Make a coarse paste of 10-12 garlic cloves, ginger, and 3 green chillies (about 2 tablespoons). Add to the onions and mix.
  6. 6.Add 1/2 tablespoon turmeric, 1/2 tablespoon salt, and 1 tablespoon red chilli powder. Add 1 chopped tomato and a little water. Cover and cook for 5 minutes.
  7. 7.Peel the roasted brinjals (skin comes off easily) and chop/mash the flesh.
  8. 8.When the masala is cooked with oil separating on top, add the mashed brinjal. Mix well.
  9. 9.Add chopped fresh coriander, 1/2 tablespoon garam masala. Cover and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  10. 10.Serve hot with roti or rice.

Tips

  • *Rubbing oil on brinjal before roasting makes the skin peel off easily
  • *Roast the brinjal while preparing the masala to save time
  • *Use a wire rack on the stove for easier roasting, or roast directly on the flame
  • *Adjust red chilli powder to your spice preference
