Hara Bhara Kabab

Restaurant-style hara bhara kabab made with spinach, peas, and paneer. Soft insi...

15-18 servings
30 min + 20 min

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch spinach (palak), washed and cleaned
  • 1 bowl green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (for boiling)
  • Chilled water (ice bath)
  • 1 bowl cabbage, chopped in a chopper
  • 2 capsicums (shimla mirch), chopped in a chopper
  • 1 tablespoon ginger garlic paste
  • 1/2 tablespoon salt (for vegetables)
  • 3 boiled potatoes, mashed
  • 200g paneer (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin powder
  • 1 tablespoon chaat masala
  • 1/2 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1/2 tablespoon salt (for batter)
  • Chopped fresh coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ginger garlic paste (for batter)
  • 1/2 tablespoon green chilli (chopped)
  • 2-3 tablespoons cornflour
  • 2 tablespoons bread crumbs (from oven-toasted bread)
  • Cashews (halved, for garnish)
  • Oil for shallow frying
  • 1-2 teaspoons oil (for cooking vegetables)
  • For chutney: 2 fistfuls coriander, 1 fistful mint, 3-4 green chillies, a small piece of ginger, 5-6 garlic cloves, 1/2 tablespoon salt, a little water
  • Yogurt (for serving chutney)
  • A little sugar (for chutney)

Directions

  1. 1.Boil water in a pot. Add spinach and peas with 1/2 teaspoon salt. Cover and boil for 5 minutes. Do not overcook.
  2. 2.Transfer the boiled spinach and peas into chilled water for 2-3 minutes to stop cooking and preserve colour.
  3. 3.Drain well, squeeze out excess water by hand, and grind into a coarse paste in a mixer (not too fine).
  4. 4.Heat 1-2 teaspoons oil in a pan. Add chopped cabbage and cook until soft. Add 1 tablespoon ginger garlic paste and fry for 1 minute.
  5. 5.Add chopped capsicum and 1/2 tablespoon salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  6. 6.Add the spinach-peas paste and mix. Cook for 2-3 minutes on low flame. Turn off heat and let it cool.
  7. 7.To the cooled mixture, add coriander powder, cumin powder, chaat masala, black pepper, 1/2 tablespoon salt, chopped coriander, 3 mashed boiled potatoes, 1 teaspoon ginger garlic paste, 1/2 tablespoon green chilli, 200g paneer (optional), 2-3 tablespoons cornflour, and 2 tablespoons bread crumbs. Mix thoroughly.
  8. 8.Oil your palms, shape into round patties, and press half a cashew on top of each kabab.
  9. 9.Heat a tawa (flat pan) with a little oil. Shallow fry kababs on low flame for about 5 minutes per side until crispy. Flip gently as they are soft.
  10. 10.For chutney: blend coriander, mint, green chillies, ginger, garlic, and salt with a little water. Mix 2 teaspoons of this paste into a bowl of yogurt. Add a little sugar for a sweet-tangy taste.
  11. 11.Serve kababs hot with the yogurt-green chutney.

Tips

  • *Transfer boiled spinach to chilled water immediately to retain the green colour
  • *Do not grind the spinach-peas paste too fine — keep it coarse
  • *Adding paneer is optional but it enhances the taste
  • *Oil your palms before shaping to prevent sticking
  • *Shallow fry on low flame and flip gently — these kababs are delicate
