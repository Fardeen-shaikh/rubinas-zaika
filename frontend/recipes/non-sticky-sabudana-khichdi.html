Non-Sticky Sabudana Khichdi

Quick and easy non-sticky sabudana khichdi, a popular fasting and breakfast reci...

3-4 servings
3 hrs 15 min + 10 min

Ingredients

  • 400g sabudana (sago/tapioca pearls), washed and soaked
  • Peanuts, roasted and coarsely crushed
  • Fresh coriander
  • Curry leaves
  • Salt - 1.5 teaspoons
  • Green chillies, finely chopped (to taste)
  • Mustard seeds (rai)
  • Cumin - 1 teaspoon
  • Oil
  • Potatoes, cut into small pieces
  • A little sugar
  • A little water

Directions

  1. 1.Wash sabudana 2-3 times with water. Soak in water for 15 minutes, then drain and let it sit (covered) for 3 hours until the pearls swell and become soft.
  2. 2.Roast peanuts and crush them coarsely. Finely chop green chillies.
  3. 3.Heat oil in a pan on full flame. Add 1 teaspoon cumin and mustard seeds. Let them splutter.
  4. 4.Add small potato pieces. Cover and cook for 2 minutes until potatoes are soft.
  5. 5.Add finely chopped green chillies (adjust to taste). Mix well.
  6. 6.Add the soaked sabudana. Mix gently on medium flame. Add 1.5 teaspoons salt.
  7. 7.Cover and cook for 1 minute. Then add 1 tablespoon roasted crushed peanuts.
  8. 8.Add a little sugar for taste. Add fresh chopped coriander and mix well.
  9. 9.Add a little water, cover, and cook on medium flame for 2 more minutes.
  10. 10.Add a teaspoon of oil and gently mix. The sabudana should become translucent and fluffy. Serve hot.

Tips

  • *Wash sabudana 2-3 times, soak for 15 minutes, then drain and rest for 3 hours — this is the key to non-sticky khichdi
  • *You can use raw or boiled potatoes — raw potatoes cook quickly when cut small
  • *Do not stir too vigorously — sabudana is delicate
  • *A little sugar enhances the overall taste
