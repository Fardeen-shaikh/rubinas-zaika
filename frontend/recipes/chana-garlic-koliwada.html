Chana Garlic Koliwada

Crispy and spicy chana koliwada - chickpeas coated in a flavourful schezwan batt...

4-5 servings
7 hrs + 20 min

Ingredients

  • 400g chickpeas (chana), soaked for 7 hours
  • Salt - 1 teaspoon (for boiling)
  • Cornflour - 3 tablespoons
  • Rice flour - 2 tablespoons
  • Kashmiri red chilli powder - 1 teaspoon
  • Spicy chilli powder - 1 teaspoon
  • Salt - 1 teaspoon
  • Cumin powder - 1 teaspoon
  • Ginger paste - 1 teaspoon
  • Garlic paste - 1 teaspoon
  • Schezwan chutney - 2 teaspoons
  • Dark soy sauce - 1 teaspoon
  • Food colour - 1 teaspoon
  • 3 heads of garlic cloves
  • Oil for deep frying
  • For dipping: 3 tablespoons tomato sauce + 2 tablespoons schezwan chutney

Directions

  1. 1.Pressure cook soaked chickpeas with water (enough to cover) and 1 teaspoon salt for 3-4 whistles. They should be cooked but not mushy. Drain and let cool for 10 minutes.
  2. 2.Make the batter: mix cornflour, rice flour, kashmiri red chilli, spicy chilli powder, salt, cumin powder, ginger paste, garlic paste, schezwan chutney, dark soy sauce, and food colour. Add water gradually to make a thick batter.
  3. 3.Add the cooled boiled chickpeas to the batter and mix well so each chickpea is coated with masala.
  4. 4.Heat oil for deep frying. First fry the garlic cloves (slit in half) for about 1 minute on medium flame until crispy. Remove and set aside.
  5. 5.Drop the battered chickpeas one by one into hot oil. Don't overcrowd. Fry on medium flame for about 1-1.5 minutes until crispy.
  6. 6.Remove and drain on tissue paper. Fry in batches.
  7. 7.For the dipping sauce, mix tomato sauce and schezwan chutney together.

Tips

  • *The batter should be thick so it coats the chickpeas well
  • *Add water gradually to avoid making the batter too thin
  • *Don't overcrowd the oil while frying
  • *Fry garlic separately for crispy texture
