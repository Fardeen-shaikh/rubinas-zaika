Mutton Dum Biryani

Authentic kacche gosht ki biryani made with raw marinated mutton layered with ba...

6-8 servings
1 hr + 45 min

Ingredients

  • Mutton
  • 5 onions, sliced
  • Ginger garlic paste - 2 tablespoons each
  • 400g yogurt (sour)
  • Ground garam masala (cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, black pepper)
  • Whole garam masala
  • 3 lemons
  • 10-15 green chillies
  • Fresh mint (pudina)
  • Fresh coriander (hara dhania)
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander powder - 2 tablespoons
  • Basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • Whole spices for rice - bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, black pepper, cumin, cardamom
  • Oil - 2 tablespoons (for marinade) + 3 tablespoons (for layering)
  • Food colour dissolved in water
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder (for layering)

Directions

  1. 1.Marinate the mutton with ginger garlic paste, salt, sour yogurt, chopped coriander, mint, coriander powder, green chillies (slit), sliced onions, lemon juice, garam masala powder, and 2 tablespoons oil. Mix very well, pressing the masala into the meat. Marinate for at least 1 hour.
  2. 2.Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Boil water with whole spices (bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, black pepper, cumin, cardamom), 2 teaspoons salt, half a lemon's juice, and 1 teaspoon oil.
  3. 3.Add soaked rice to boiling water and cook until 70-80% done (rice should break when pressed). Drain the water.
  4. 4.In a heavy-bottomed pot, add 2 tablespoons oil. Layer some boiled rice on the bottom to prevent sticking.
  5. 5.Add all the marinated mutton on top of the rice layer and spread evenly.
  6. 6.Top with chopped coriander, mint, fried onions, and remaining boiled rice.
  7. 7.Drizzle 3 tablespoons oil, sprinkle garam masala powder, and add food colour dissolved in water at various spots.
  8. 8.Seal the pot tightly with aluminium foil or a cotton cloth. Place the lid on top.
  9. 9.Cook on high flame for 15 minutes, then transfer to a tawa (griddle) and cook on low flame for 20 minutes.
  10. 10.Turn off the gas and let it rest covered for 10 minutes. Gently mix and serve with raita.

Tips

  • *Always use sour yogurt for the best marinade
  • *No red chilli powder or turmeric goes in this biryani
  • *Use aluminium foil or cotton cloth to seal the pot for proper dum
  • *Place a heavy weight on the lid to prevent steam from escaping
  • *Marinate for at least 1 hour for best flavour
