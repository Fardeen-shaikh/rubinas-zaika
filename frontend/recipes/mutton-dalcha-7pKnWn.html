Mutton Dalcha

Authentic Muslim-style mutton dalcha with toor and masoor dal, bottle gourd, tom...

6-8 servings
45 min + 45 min

Ingredients

  • For dal: 1 bowl toor dal + 1/2 bowl masoor dal, soaked 30 min
  • Salt - 1/2 tablespoon, Oil - 1/2 tablespoon (for dal)
  • 3/4 kg mutton, washed
  • For mutton: coriander powder, turmeric, ginger garlic paste, salt, 1 small onion
  • 1 large bottle gourd (dudhi/lauki), cut into large pieces
  • 3 large tomatoes, sliced
  • Fresh coriander and 4-5 green chillies
  • 2 large onions, sliced
  • Ginger garlic paste - 1 tablespoon
  • Red chilli powder, turmeric, salt, cumin, coriander powder - 1 tablespoon each
  • Tamarind, soaked
  • Whole spices: cinnamon, shahjeera, black pepper, 10-12 green cardamom (ground coarsely)
  • Oil - 1 tablespoon
  • For dalcha rice: 1 kg rice, 3 tablespoons oil, 1 onion, ginger garlic paste
  • Whole spices for rice, green chillies, coriander, salt

Directions

  1. 1.Wash and soak dals for 30 minutes. Pressure cook with 2 glasses water, salt, and oil for 4-5 whistles.
  2. 2.Pressure cook mutton with coriander powder, turmeric, ginger garlic paste, salt, sliced onion, and 1 glass water for 3-4 whistles.
  3. 3.In a large pot, heat oil. Fry onions until brown. Add ginger garlic paste and fry.
  4. 4.Add all dry spices. Add bottle gourd, tomatoes, coriander, and green chillies. Mix well, cover and cook 2-3 minutes without water.
  5. 5.Add the cooked mutton (without stock). Mix well and cook 2-3 minutes.
  6. 6.Add the cooked dal and mix well. Add tamarind pulp and mutton stock.
  7. 7.Add warm water to desired consistency (keep it thick). Add the coarsely ground whole spice powder.
  8. 8.Adjust salt and spices. Simmer on low flame for 10 minutes.
  9. 9.For dalcha rice: In a cooker, fry onion in oil, add ginger garlic paste, whole spices, green chillies, coriander. Add washed rice, double water, salt. Cook for 3 whistles (2 high, 1 low).

Tips

  • *Soaking dals prevents acidity
  • *Add warm water only, never cold
  • *Thick dalcha tastes better than thin
  • *Tamarind adds the signature tangy flavour
